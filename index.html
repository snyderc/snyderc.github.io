About Me

 

In high school, my website was #1 on Google (for the admittedly noncompetitive keyword “miniature golf in CT”) with a few hundred unique visitors each summer day, and I was making my own simple TI-83 graphing calculator programs.

Not realizing that programming could be a career choice, I instead studied politics, learning enough to know I didn’t want to go into politics! So for the past few years, I’ve edited two books about product creation for startups, moved 20 people and 4 vacuum chambers across town, and run logistics for events keynoted by big-name Boston-area tech founders.

And along the way, I created a database to automate colleagues’ expense reporting, modified a web scraper so that I could customize how a third-party program displayed its data, and learned how to get an admin account on a WordPress site via MySQL commands. No matter where I went, I saw the power of tech.

But I never forgot the excitement of my undergraduate programming class where I pulled an all-nighter to finish the final project… a full week before it was actually due. So I’ve brushed up on modern languages and libraries and gotten to work on some nonprofit and personal projects.

I like getting the details right and figuring out how to make my users’ lives easier. How can I help you?
